# Ottoman Rule in Lebanon (1516–1918)

## 1. Start of Ottoman Control
- The Ottomans defeated the Mamluks in 1516.
- Lebanon became part of the Ottoman Empire with partial local autonomy.
- Mountain regions like Mount Lebanon stayed semi-independent because they were hard to control.

## 2. The Ma‘an & Shihab Dynasties
- The Ma‘an family ruled Mount Lebanon first — including Fakhr al-Din II, who modernized the region and made ties with Europe.
- After them, the Shihab family took over.
- The Shihabs shifted political influence toward the Maronite Christian community.

## 3. Sectarian Tensions
- Maronites, Druze, and Muslims lived close together.
- European powers supported different groups, increasing tensions.
- Conflicts rose especially in the 19th century.

## 4. 1860 Civil Conflict
- Violent clashes broke out between Druze and Maronites.
- France intervened to protect Maronites.
- This forced the Ottomans to reorganize Lebanon’s governance.

## 5. Mutasarrifate of Mount Lebanon (1861–1918)
- After the conflict, the Ottomans created the **Mutasarrifate** system.
- Lebanon was ruled by a Christian governor chosen with approval from European powers.
- This era brought stability, economic development, and early self-rule structures.
