# Lebanese Civil War (1975â€“1990)

## 1. Causes
- Sectarian tensions between Christians, Muslims, and Druze.
- Palestinian presence and militias in Lebanon.
- Political imbalance and corruption.
- Regional conflicts involving Israel, Syria, and others.

## 2. Major Phases
- 1975: War begins with clashes in Beirut.
- 1976: Syrian army intervenes.
- 1982: Israeli invasion of southern Lebanon.
- 1989: **Taif Agreement** negotiated to end hostilities.
- 1990: War officially ends.

## 3. Consequences
- Around 150,000 people killed.
- Massive destruction of Beirut and other cities.
- Syrian military and political influence increased in Lebanon.
- Economic collapse and mass emigration.

## 4. Key Notes
- Sectarian divisions deepened.
- Many neighborhoods became segregated along religious lines.
- Reconstruction started post-1990, but scars remain.
