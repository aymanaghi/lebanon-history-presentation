# French Mandate of Lebanon (1920–1943)

## 1. How It Started
- After WWI, the Ottoman Empire collapsed.
- France took control of Lebanon under the League of Nations mandate.
- In 1920, France officially created **Greater Lebanon**, adding new regions to Mount Lebanon.

## 2. Borders & New Identity
- The new Lebanon included:
  - Beirut
  - Tripoli
  - Bekaa Valley
  - South Lebanon
  - North Lebanon
- This mix created Lebanon’s modern religious diversity.

## 3. Constitution of 1926
- Lebanon adopted its first constitution.
- Set up:
  - A President (always Maronite)
  - A Parliament
  - A form of democracy inspired by France
- This started Lebanon’s modern political system.

## 4. Economic & Cultural Changes
- France built:
  - Roads
  - Schools
  - Administrative systems
- French language became widely used.
- Beirut grew into a financial and cultural center.

## 5. Rising Nationalism
- Many Lebanese wanted independence.
- Major protests happened in 1936 and 1943.
- Lebanese leaders pushed hard to end French control.

## 6. Independence
- In 1943, Lebanese leaders created the **National Pact**, a power-sharing agreement.
- France imprisoned the Lebanese president and ministers → huge national outrage.
